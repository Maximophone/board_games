Corporate Warfare

Objective

From an initial capital, build a company become the first one to multiply your investment. Players act as CEOs of rival corporations, building their empire while engaging in corporate espionage.

Components

  • Pen and Paper: For tracking resources, departments, and actions.
  • A Modified Playing Cards Deck: Use only the face cards (A, K, Q, J) and two Jokers. These are the "Special Action" cards.
  • 6-Sided Dice: For introducing elements of chance and uncertainty. The more dice the better.
  • Tokens of three types: for representing Products, Employees, and Dollars.

Setup

  • Each player starts with:
    • 3 Employee tokens
    • A hand of 2 Special Action cards drawn from the modified deck.
    • 10 dollar tokens
  • Players have four departments: Research, HR, Production, and Sales. These can be drawn as rectangles on a piece of paper
  • Determine the first player via a coin toss or another fair method.

Turn Structure

Each turn consists of four phases:

  1. Allocation Phase
    • Players allocate their available employee tokens among the different departments.
    • Note: One employee token cannot be split between multiple departments.
  2. Action Phase
    • Players may play any number of Special Action cards from their hand.
    • Includes playing an "Espionage" card, which — if successful — disrupts a chosen department in the opponent's corporation for the current turn. (See Espionage section for details.)
  3. Execution Phase
    • Roll a die for the overall market demand for the current turn. Add 2 for each "Market Research" card in play.
    • For each department where they have employee tokens, players roll a 6-sided die per employee:
      • Production: On a 4-6, gain a product token.
      • Research: On a 4-6, draw a special action card. If there is no card left in the pile, the discard is shuffled and becomes the new pile.
      • HR: On a 4-6, gain a new employee token, if desired.
      • Sales: On a 4-6, sell a product for 3 money tokens, up to the overall market demand. For example if the demand is 2, each player can sell at most two products. The products sold are discarded.
    • When an "Improved Methods" card is placed on a department, it is upgraded, lowering the success roll threshold (from 4-6 to 3-6, 3-6 to 2-6, etc.).
  4. Economic Upkeep Phase
    • Players must pay 1 money token per 3 employees for upkeep costs.
    • Players must pay 1 money token per 6 products they have for storage costs.
    • If a player cannot afford upkeep for all their employees, they must let one go.
    • Players can fire any number of employees if they wish to
    • At the end of the turn, players can have a maximum of 5 cards in their hand. They discard any additional card of their choosing.


Special Action Cards

  • Espionage (Joker): Disrupts a chosen department in the opponent's corporation for one turn. Discard after use.
  • Reconversion (Queen): Reallocate one employee to another department permanently. Discard after use.
  • Innovation Boost (King): Doubles the output of the Department of your choice for one turn. Place this card face-up on the upgraded department. Discard after use.
  • Improved Methods (Ace): Upgrades a department, making success rolls easier (see execution phase). Can be cumulated. Place this card face-up on the upgraded department.
  • Market Research (J): When played, adds 2 permanently to the overall demand (see execution phase). Place this card face-up in the center of the board.

Espionage Mechanic

  • Players may bluff that they have an Espionage card, even if they don't.
  • To play Espionage, place a card face-down and target a department.
  • The targeted player can either call the bluff or accept the sabotage. If they accept, the sabotage is successful and the face-down card is discarded.
  • If a bluff is called and revealed:
    • If it's an Espionage card, the defender loses an employee token in the targeted department and the sabotage is successful.
    • If it's not an Espionage card, the attacker rolls a die and loses that amount of money. If they can't pay, they lose an employee token.

Winning Condition

The game concludes when any player has more than 30 dollars at the end of a round, after paying its employees. The player with the most money tokens wins.
